Candy Coating Chocolate

Candy coating chocolate, also known as compound chocolate or confectionery coating, is a type of chocolate that is made with vegetable oil instead of cocoa butter. This gives it a smooth texture and allows it to be melted and used for coating candy and other treats.

Candy coating chocolate typically has a lower percentage of cocoa solids than traditional chocolate, ranging from about 20% to 50%. It also contains sugar, milk powder, and other ingredients that give it a sweet flavor and creamy texture. It is available in a variety of colors and flavors, making it popular for creating colorful and fun confections.

One advantage of candy coating chocolate is that it is easier to work with than traditional chocolate. It melts more easily and doesn't require tempering, which can be a difficult and time-consuming process. Candy coating chocolate also has a longer shelf life than traditional chocolate and can be stored at room temperature without the risk of blooming or melting.

While candy coating chocolate is a popular choice for candy making and decorating, it is not considered real chocolate due to the absence of cocoa butter. However, it can be a delicious and convenient alternative for those who want to create sweet treats at home.
